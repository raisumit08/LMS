Leave Management Policy


1. Purpose:


The Leave Management Policy of our Organization aims to establish guidelines and procedures for managing employee leaves efficiently and fairly. This policy aims to promote work-life balance for employees while ensuring the smooth functioning of the organization.



2. Types of Leaves:


a. Earned Leave: All regular full-time employees are entitled to [15] days of paid Earned leave per calendar year. The leave entitlement will be prorated for part-time employees based on their working hours.
b. Sick Leave: Employees are entitled to [5] days of paid sick leave per calendar year to attend to personal illness, medical appointments, or to care for immediate family members.
c. Casual Leave: Employees may request unpaid personal/casual leave for special situations not covered by other leave types. Employees are entitled to [7] days of paid



3. Leave Approval Process:


a. Employees must submit leave requests through the [Leave Management System/Application] at least [1-2] working days prior, except for emergencies.
b. HR is responsible for reviewing and approving leave requests based on business requirements and staffing needs.
c. HR should respond to leave requests promptly, either approving or rejecting the request with a valid reason.



4. Leave Balance and Tracking:


a. Employees can view their leave balance and usage through the [Leave Management System].
b. Our Organization will maintain accurate records of employee leave balances and usage.



5. Return-to-Work Policy:


a. Employees on extended leaves are required to provide a written notice of their intention to return to work at least [3] working days before their planned return date.
b. Our Organization will ensure a smooth transition for employees returning from extended leaves.



6. Policy Review:

The Leave Management Policy will be reviewed periodically to ensure its effectiveness and alignment with changing organizational needs and relevant labor laws.



Note: This is a general example of a Leave Management Policy and may need customization to suit the specific needs, legal requirements, and practices of a particular organization. It is crucial to consult with legal advisors and HR experts to ensure compliance with applicable laws and regulations.
